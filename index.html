Play-by-Play

How to Play

▾

The Goal

First team to 21 wins. Offense creates a quality shot before the 24-click clock dies; defense contests and drains the clock.

The Board

A basketball half court overlaid with a grid of tiles. The hoop is at the bottom baseline; half court is the top row. Tile tints show the regions — paint (red), corners (green), wings (blue), elbows (amber), top of key (purple).

Your Turn

  1. Offense: tap a gold piece, then tap any of the 8 highlighted tiles around it to move (costs 1 click). Or tap Shoot (free, ends the possession).
  2. Defense: tap a blue piece, tap an adjacent tile to move (1 click), then Done defending.
Pieces move like a chess king — one tile in any direction, including diagonals. Faint dotted trails trace each piece's path through the possession.

The 24-Click Clock

Both teams share one clock. Every move spends 1 click. Run it to 0 with no shot = shot-clock violation, ball turns over.

Shot Quality → Dice

Coverage is now measured in tile distance (king distance) to the nearest defender at the moment you shoot:
CoverageDice
Wide open (nearest defender 3+ tiles away)3
Open (defender 2 tiles away)2
Contested (defender 0–1 tiles away)1
Clock at 6 or below? Subtract 1 die (pressure), min 1.

Making the Shot

Your tile decides the zone, and each zone has a threshold. Roll your dice — if any die meets or beats the threshold, it's a bucket (+2). Lower threshold = better shot.

Man vs Zone

Public info — both teams see the scheme. In v0/v2 both use the same tile-distance check; the split deepens later. Switch assignment mid-possession costs 2 clicks.

Winning

Reach 21 and the game ends on that shot.
